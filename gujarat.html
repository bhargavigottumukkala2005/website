Rann of Kutch

The Rann of Kutch is a vast salt marsh located in the Thar Desert in the Kutch district of Gujarat. It is renowned for its stunning white salt desert which spans over 7,500 square kilometers, making it one of the largest salt deserts in the world. The region transforms into a surreal landscape during the Rann Utsav festival, typically held from November to February, attracting tourists from all over the world. The festival showcases the rich culture, handicrafts, music, and dance of the Kutch region.

Champaner-Pavagadh Archaeological Park

Champaner-Pavagadh Archaeological Park is a UNESCO World Heritage Site located in the Panchmahal district of Gujarat. It encompasses the historical city of Champaner and the sacred Pavagadh Hill, showcasing a blend of Hindu and Islamic architecture dating back to the 8th to 14th centuries. The park is home to numerous forts, palaces, mosques, temples, step wells, and other architectural marvels, including the Jama Masjid, Kevda Masjid, Sahar ki Masjid, and Pavagadh Fort.

Satpura

Saputara is a picturesque hill station nestled in the Sahyadri range of the Western Ghats in the Dang district of Gujarat. Known as the "Abode of Serpents," Saputara offers a refreshing escape from the plains with its cool climate, lush greenery, and serene lakes. The Saputara Lake is a popular spot for boating and leisurely walks along the promenade. Sunset Point offers breathtaking views of the surrounding hills and valleys.
